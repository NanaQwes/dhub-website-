Welcome To University Of Cape Coast Design Thinking And Innovation Hub (D-HUB)

Unemployment is a global issue that affects both developed and underdeveloped countries. Data from the Institute of Statistics, Social and Economic Research (ISSER) of the University of Ghana, has revealed that only 10 per cent of graduates find jobs after their first year of completing school

It is against this backdrop that the Design Thinking and Innovation Hub (D-Hub) was established under the Office of the former Vice Chancellor of the University of Cape Coast (UCC), Proffesor Johnson Nyarko Boampong.The D-Hub aims at creating a hub that gives faculty, staff and students opportunities to explore how design thinking might support teaching, scholarship and service. The hub also incubates innovative ideas of students, lecturers, and the community to grow them into viable businesses through entrepreneurship, innovation and continuous improvement.

Mandate Of D-HUB

The mandates of the D-Hub are:

  • Professional skills development
  • Business development
  • Incubation and Acceleration of innovative ideas
  • Career support and community service

The Hub was officially launched on Tuesday 5th April, 2022.

Purpose Of D-HUB

At D-Hub, our purpose is to contribute to national development through entrepreneurship and career development support targeting the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

  • 4: Quality Education
  • 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth
  • 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Goals Of D-HUB

To facilitate the development of entrepreneurship and career development by resourcing, training, coaching and mentoring staff, sevice providers and beneficiaries.

Objectives Of D-HUB

Our main objectives are:

  • to develop a good governance framework for effective planning and efficient implementation of the Hub's activities.
  • to facilitate the improvement and development of requisite competencies, products and services and infrastructure for the delivery of the activities of the Hub.
  • to facilitate the assessment, provision, delivery and monitoring of entrepreneurship, employeeship and freelancing resource packages to students, staff and community.

KIC Agritech Challenge (Classic) 2024 – March

The AgriTech Challenge Classic is a 7-month annual training program aimed at developing the interest and building the mindset of students and young graduates to ultimately drive systemic change in Ghana’s agriculture sector. It provides the youth with the right exposure to the agriculture value chain and provides networking opportunity to build and grow viable solutions to Ghana’s key agricultural challenges.

The University Challenge

A University Challenge student is expected - alongside a team of their interconnected peers - to develop an incubator for theoretical solutions and apply them to real world problems. This event will be a national project and a competition amongst 12 public and private universities. Authorities of respective universities are to elect talents and individuals across faculties and halls to represent the university. A research project topic will be given to each university to be researched by these groups and presented to a group of judges. The best five (5) winning projects will be selected out of the twelve (12) competing universities and an overall winner will be selected from five (5).

Resource Packages

In the next couple of months, the D-Hub will roll out the under-listed resource packages.

  • Industry-Led Master Classes

    • The purpose of the masterclass is to create a platform for participants to engage on a personal level with experts in different fields to understand career decision, career path and lessons learnt (industry needs, how to grow and rise) etc. This will be held monthly in each semester.

  • Entrepreneurship Incubation

    • this is aimed at giving start-ups access to mentorship, investors and other support to help them get established. To be eligible for this, the startup must be running and registered. It is for a period of 1 to 2 years

  • Career Incubation

    • this is aimed at giving training, coaching and mentoring to help individuals be employable.

  • Entrepreneurship and Career Support Walk-in Clinics

    • the D-Hub plans to provide a platform where persons with specialized knowledge in entrepreneurship and career development provide advice and support to students and staff. Experts will be from areas such as legal, accounting and finance, entrepreneurship, career and marketing.
